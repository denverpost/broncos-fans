  • Mile High Mascot

    Mile High Mascot, Bucky: Denver’s Bronco

    Denver Post sports columnist Woody Paige pauses to reflect on Bucky, the Denver Broncos ’ mascot. Bucky is a 27-foot tall, 1,600-pound statue made of fiberglass and metal that stands atop Sports Authority Field at Mile High's south scoreboard. Moved from the old Mile High Stadium, Bucky has seen every down played by the Broncos since he was originally sculpted in 1975. Bucky was removed from the former stadium in May 2001, and waited three months before being installed in his new home.

  • Memorabilia King

    Denver Dale, King of Denver Broncos Memorabilia

    Dale Turner is affectionately referred to by his family and friends as ldquo;Denver Dale.rdquo; He moved back to Colorado from California a decade ago to be closer to his beloved team, but never missed an opportunity to see the Broncos play in San Diego, or Oakland. He started collecting Broncos memorabilia when he was 11, in the 1960’s, because of his love for the Denver ldquo;Drdquo; logo. Growing up in the shadows of Bear Stadium, Denver Dale has never let anything, not even a colon cancer diagnosis three years ago, stop him from spending his Sundays with his football team.

  • Finding Broncos Country

    Finding Broncos Country

    The Denver Broncos are the football team their reigns in the Rocky Mountain Empire. The Denver Post went out on a walkabout of the region to find Broncos fanatics, the people that make the games happen every week at Sports Authority Field at Mile High, fans lost in other cities, tailgaters and podcasters. DPtv Videographer Vince Chandler picked up his camera on gameday and headed the opposite direction of the players. People know the players, they know the ownership and management, but who’s responsible for painting the field? Who is playing the music that is heard floating through tailgate lots or the stadium as fans come in? Broncos Country is a large, untamed wilderness of blue and orange, and collected here are a few moments in time in the greatest football fandom in the world.

  • Football’s Flag

    Football’s Flag, A Mile High salute

    The Denver Broncos begin every home game the way most sports teams in the United States of America does, with the singing of the National Anthem. A colorguard is present, flags are shown on screens and waved by fans, but none harder than the 60-yard flag that over 200 volunteers open every home game Sunday. The 200 are found through the Broncos’ season ticket holders, and for many of them, the question is: Which is more exciting, standing on their team’s field or waving their country’s flag?

  • Lil’ Leaders

    The Denver Broncos Junior Cheerleaders: Lil’ Leaders

    For more than twenty years the Denver Broncos Junior Cheerleaders have joined the Broncos’ Cheerleaders on the sidelines at Mile High Stadium to help cheer their team on to victory. On November 29, 2015 in the snow and against Tom Brady and the New England Patriots, more than 500 Colorado girls rush on to the field at Mile High, ‘Smeyes’ blazing, ready to roar.

  • Broncos ’N Booze

    Broncos ’N Booze: Talking Denver Broncos from coast to coast

    DJ Bedz has the immense responsibility of being the person to set the mood at Sports Authority Field at Mile High every week for the Broncos to take the field to. He spins a mix of the Denver Broncos player’s requests, old favorites, and new tunes, working to build the perfect mix tape, for every single home game. His mix is what Broncos fans listen to, sing along to, and prepare themselves to cheer their team on to victory. Go behind the scenes with DPtv as Denver Post visual journalist Vince Chandler takes the field with DJ Bedz.

  • Broncos’ Brass

    Denver Broncos’ Brass: The Mile High Marching Band

    The Stampede Drum Line and the Broncos Brass have been a staple sound at Sports Authority Field at Mile High for years. Started as the Stampede Drum Line in 2003, the group was one of the first NFL drum lines, a number that has now moved in to the low twenties. Tailgaters can hear their favorite pep numbers played by the professional musicians, and fans in the stadium start every game with the band playing the Denver Broncos’ fight song.

  • Mutton Busters

    Buckin’ Broncos, Mutton Busters

    Buckin’ Broncos, Mutton Busters Colorado is known nationwide for several things, and topping most lists would be the Denver Broncos, and rodeos. Namely, the National Western Stock Show. For the first time ever, in the Super Bowl’s 50th year, and the National Western’s 110th, the dynamic duo have come together, bringing mutton busting to Sports Authority Field at Mile High. On December 28, 2015 at halftime of the last Monday Night Football game of the season, the National Western crew pulled up to the North loading docks of Sports Authority Field, and unloaded a trailer of sheep onto the Buckin Broncos’ field.

  • Best Fans

    Broncos Country: The Best Fans in Football

    The Denver Broncos are proud to say they have sold out their home Sports Authority Field at Mile High Stadium for more than 300-straight games, a streak they call the second longest in professional sports. This is because around the country, the Broncos have some of the best fans in football. At the end of the stadium, for the final home game of the 2015 season the Broncos went above and beyond to show their fans how much they truly appreciate them.

    The Mad Fanatic

    When it comes to hip-hop, most emcees worth their salt keep it real, and write rhymes about what they know. For Andrew Young, a.k.a.
